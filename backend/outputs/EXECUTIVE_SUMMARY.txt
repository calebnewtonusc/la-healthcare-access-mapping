Executive Summary for Decision Makers

KEY FINDINGS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
• Total population affected by access gaps: 3,007,726
• Critical priorities identified: 2
• Total recommendations: 5

CRITICAL PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Build Healthcare Facilities in Extreme Access Deserts
   Category: Infrastructure
   Population Impact: 80,831 people
   Cost Estimate: Very High
   Timeline: Medium-term
   Description: Identified 25 areas where residents are more than 10km from the nearest healthcare facility, affecting 80,831 people.
   Expected Impact: Reduce average travel distance by 40-60% for affected populations

   First Steps:
   → Conduct detailed land use and zoning analysis
   → Engage with community stakeholders
   → Secure funding through state/federal grants

2. Prioritize Healthcare Investments in Low-Income Areas
   Category: Equity
   Population Impact: 1,436 people
   Cost Estimate: High
   Timeline: Medium-term
   Description: Identified 2 low-income census tracts with poor healthcare access requiring priority intervention.
   Expected Impact: Reduce healthcare disparities and improve health equity

   First Steps:
   → Allocate additional funding to safety-net providers
   → Expand Medicaid/Medicare coverage and enrollment
   → Provide free or subsidized preventive care

HIGH PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Deploy Mobile Health Clinics to Underserved Communities
   Category: Service Expansion
   Population Impact: 4,805 people
   Cost Estimate: Medium
   Timeline: Short-term
   Description: Implement mobile clinic program targeting 3 vulnerable areas with limited transportation and poor access.
   Expected Impact: Immediate access improvement for vulnerable populations without requiring new infrastructure

   First Steps:
   → Establish rotating schedule for mobile clinics
   → Partner with local community centers and schools
   → Provide basic primary care, vaccinations, and screenings

2. Expand Healthcare Transportation Services
   Category: Transportation
   Population Impact: 2,916,631 people
   Cost Estimate: Low
   Timeline: Immediate
   Description: Over 10% of households in 776 census tracts lack vehicle access, creating significant barriers to healthcare.
   Expected Impact: Reduce transportation barriers for 50,000+ residents

   First Steps:
   → Expand medical transport voucher programs
   → Partner with ride-sharing services for subsidized healthcare trips
   → Add bus routes connecting to major medical centers

1. Expand Telehealth Services in Low-Access Areas
   Category: Service Expansion
   Population Impact: 4,023 people
   Cost Estimate: Low
   Timeline: Short-term
   Description: Leverage telehealth to improve access for 2 areas with poor physical access scores.
   Expected Impact: Provide immediate access to primary care and specialists

   First Steps:
   → Provide subsidized internet/devices for low-income families
   → Set up telehealth kiosks at libraries and community centers
   → Train healthcare providers on telehealth best practices
